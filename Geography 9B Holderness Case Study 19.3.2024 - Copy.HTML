The Russian 1905 Revolution





Russian Government




Nicholas Romanov (Nicholas II) was the autocrat of the Russian Empire, meaning he had absolute power over the country. He was advised by his ministers, who were chosen from the aristocracy and senior church leaders.





Reasons for discontent with the Tsar



Over three-quarters of the population were discontented with their position in the empire.


Peasants
 Peasants made up approximately 85% of the population. The peasants wanted land due to their growing population but most were too poor to buy it. There were famines in 1901 and the 1890s that killed thousands of peasants. Despite them not making enough food for themselves, they were expected to produce extra grain for exports.

Industrial workers
 Factory conditions were dangerous, trade unions were banned, wages were low, rules were enforced strictly and there was little legislation to protect the safety and pay of workers. The working day was also 11.5 hours long. Due to a housing shortage, many workers were forced to live in overcrowded, dirty barracks where disease spread rapidly and they shared kitchens and toilets. Others slept in the factory.







Bloody Sunday


Father Geoge Gapon led a peaceful protest of 150,000 unarmed people in St Petersburg at 6 assembly points at the Winter Palace. This was to present a petition to the tsar that the demonstrators signed that requested an 8-hour working day, the legalization of trade unions, the right to strike and the formation of a secret ballot and universal suffrage. The tsar had fled to his palace in Tsarskoye Selo. The protestors shouted insults at the soldiers. The soldiers blocked the protestors from the palace and fired 2 warning shots, used their whips and then their swords. Eventually, the soldiers opened fire, killing over 100 and wounding hundreds more. Nicholas II didn’t order the guards to open fire but many people blamed him for it. Overall, the massacre united people against the tsarist government.








October Manifesto



The October Manifesto (officially, The Manifesto on the Improvement of the State Order) was a document published by the tsar on 17th October 1905. It granted:

• Freedom of speech and religion and the right to form political parties and trade unions

• The formation of the duma. Any laws must be approved by the duma and government actions would be supervised by its elected representatives.

The duma satisfied the liberal middle class and the general strike was called off. However, it did not help peasants, workers or soldiers. Peasant and worker unrest continued.









Why did Nicholas II survive in 1905?


There are 3 major reasons why the tsar was not overthrown during the political unrest in 1905:

The October Manifesto

The October Manifesto satisfied the liberal middle class, so it reduced pressure on the tsar.

The Military

The Tsar could rely on the armed forces to stop any unrest. Mutinies were unusual and Nicholas took a loan from France to pay his troops and improve loyalty among the military.

Disunited opposition

The demands of the tsars’ opponents were disunited. Peasants wanted more land, people in the city wanted cheaper bread and workers wanted better wages and improved working conditions. Furthermore, protests were spontaneous and so ineffective. Unrest was mostly about economic issues as well, instead of a specific political goal.
